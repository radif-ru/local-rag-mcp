# General Guidelines

At Sytac, we aim to do what’s right—for our clients, our colleagues, and our community. These guidelines promote respect, openness, and collaboration, helping everyone feel safe to speak up, ask questions, and learn. They’re not rigid rules, but principles for working together with empathy, transparency, and a long-term view. By following them, we make Sytac a place where trust grows and great work happens.

1. **Act with integrity** – Always do what’s right for clients, colleagues, and the community; think long-term and never act unethically.
2. **Respect everyone** – No jokes or remarks about race, gender, sexual orientation, or other personal attributes.
3. **Communicate openly** – Share problems as they arise, be forthcoming with information, ask for help when needed, and clearly state the next action. Encourage a culture where it’s safe to say “I don’t know” or “I don’t understand.”
4. **Own and learn from mistakes** – If you make a mistake, don’t worry, correct it and proactively let the affected party and your team know what happened and how you fixed it. Improve processes to prevent recurrence.
5. **Work together constructively** – Remind each other of guidelines without judgment, resolve disagreements respectfully (escalate if needed without repercussions).
6. **Focus on outcomes** – Output matters more than constant activity; personal breaks, private browsing, or personal calls are fine when balanced with results. Everyone at the company cares about your **output**. Being away from the keyboard during the workday, doing private browsing, or making personal phone calls is fine and encouraged.
7. **Be transparent and proactive** – Share work proactively, be honest about workload and deadlines. If you are unhappy with anything (your duties, your colleague, your manager, your salary, your location, your computer) please let your Consultant Manager know as soon as you realize it. We want to solve problems while they are **small**.
8. **Follow good practices** – Think strategically about every action, respect code licenses, document decisions promptly, and contact your Consultant Manager anytime for clarity.
9. **Support the community**: We want to have a great company so if you meet people that are ****better ****than yourself try to recruit them to join us.
10. If you have questions about anything written in this handbook you can always contact your [Consultant Manager](https://www.notion.so/People-Operations-2f0e1020820c4454a63bcb19306a35d4?pvs=21).
