# Standby & Overtime

It may be possible that the client you work for asks you to participate in a team schedule standby shift to deal with a variety of issues that can arise outside of normal working hours.

For the purposes of this policy, the term ‘standby’ is defined as the time during which employees need to be available to respond to a telephone call from the client quickly and be in a state of readiness to perform work as required.

Employees who undertake ‘standby’ duty are therefore required to be available to receive and deal with work-related issues for the whole duration of a pre-determined period outside normal working hours.

While being on standby you are expected able to be ready to work within generally an hour. This means you can still go on about your day but are expected to be available for impediments.

### **Monetization**

During your standby shift, you will receive €2.74 gross per hour that you’ve been on standby on workdays, and €4.11 during the weekend and national holidays (rate for 2024). If you need to work during these hours, you will receive overtime compensation the following compensation for overtime plus your hourly bonus:  

| Working days | 115% per hour |
| --- | --- |
| Saturdays  | 125% per hour |
| Sun/holidays | 140% per hour  |

Overtime is applicable during the following hours

| Working days | 19:00 - 07:00  |
| --- | --- |
| Saturdays  | All hours |
| Sun/holidays  | All hours  |

*Note: These overtime compensations are only applicable when the client has specifically asked you to work during these hours.*

### Employee responsibilities

Whilst on standby you must not consume alcohol or take drugs that could impact your ability to respond or perform your work. If you become unwell or are unable to comply with your responsibilities during a period of standby for example due to an unexpected change in personal circumstances or ill health you must contact the nominated person as soon as possible so that alternative arrangements can be made.
