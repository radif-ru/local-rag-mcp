# Sickness

# ⚠️ **Reporting sick & recovered**

**Company Policy on Reporting Illness**

1. **Notification to Client**
    
    In the event of illness, the employee must personally report sick to their client manager **no later than 9:00 AM** on the day of absence.
    
2. **Notification to Sytac**
    
    The employee must also send an email to **office@sytac.io** and to their **consultant manager**, informing them of the situation.
    
3. **Leaving Work Due to Illness**
    
    If an employee needs to leave work during working hours because of illness, they must personally notify their immediate superior at the client **before leaving**, as well as inform **Sytac on the same day**.
    
4. **Sick Reporting Deadline**
    
    Sick reports submitted on the **next working day** will **not** be accepted, and the employee will be required to cover this day at their own cost. Exceptions may be made if the employee was **physically unable** to report their illness on time.
    
    ⚠️ If a sick leave extends beyond one working week, the employee will receive a phone call from the Case Manager at AtArbo on the 8th working day of their illness.
    

**Required Information During Reporting Sickness**

- **Probable Duration:** Provide an estimated duration of the sick leave.
- **Contact Details (if applicable):** When reporting sick while on holiday, employees must supply their accommodation address and phone number.
- **Attending Doctor:** Provide the name of the attending doctor if available.

**Confidentiality**

The company respects the privacy of employees and does not inquire about the nature of the illness. All health-related information is handled with strict confidentiality in accordance with GDPR and company policies.

This information will be passed on to the working conditions service (Arbo Services) at the first day of the employee’s absence. Furthermore, the employee and employer will make arrangements regarding the date of the return to work and/or the date and method of follow-up contact (for example, contact by phone after each visit to the attending doctor).

⚠️ If the employee has recovered, they should will inform their client manager and send an email to their Consultant Manager and [office@sytac.io](mailto:office@sytac.io) with the date of recovery.

### **Illness resulting from an industrial accident or other accident**

If an employee is involved in an accident resulting in an absence from work due to illness, the employee must report their sickness through the usual channels. The employee must clearly indicate that the illness is a result of an accident.

In certain cases, the company may be able to claim the lost wages from the relevant insurance company. Additionally, the employee is required to inform their immediate supervisor if the injury occurred as part of an industrial accident.

## **Staying at home**

After reporting sick, the employee must stay at their residence address until they have recovered or until the employer has taken further action. The employee may leave their residence only to visit a general practitioner, the occupational health service medical officer, to resume work, or with the employer’s prior consent.

## **Consulting the general practitioner**

It is in the employee’s interest to start being treated by their general practitioner or other doctors within a reasonable period of time and to follow this doctor’s instructions.

## **Make it possible to visit**

The occupational health service employee or medical officer must be able to contact the employee. This means the employee must remain reachable by phone and available for home visits or visits at their designated nursing address. If the employee is not present during a visit by the occupational health service medical officer or employer, they must ensure their current location can be determined from their residence or nursing address.

## **Correct address**

If the employee moves house during their occupational disability or is temporarily staying elsewhere or else changes his/her nursing address (for example because he/she is admitted to or discharged from a hospital or other institution) then the employee must inform their employer and the working conditions service of this within twelve hours.

## **Attending the doctor’s surgery**

The employee must comply with a request to attend the working conditions service surgery or the surgery of the specialist appointed by the employer. This request must still be complied with even if the employee intends to resume his/her work that day or subsequently. If the employee has a valid reason for non-attendance (such as being admitted to hospital) then he/she must inform the employer immediately and must also inform the working conditions service medical officer at least 48 hours beforehand.

## **Medical examination**

Under the Eligibility for Permanent Invalidity Benefit (Restrictions) Act (*Wet Verbetering Poortwachter*), the employee is obliged to cooperate with a medical examination or rehabilitation process carried out by or on the orders of the working conditions service medical officer, if this officer feels that such an examination or training is necessary.

## **Do not hinder your recovery**

If during his/her period of occupational disability the employee acts in such a way that he/she hinders his/her recovery then the employer can refuse to carry on paying his/her wages during the illness.

## Return to work upon recovery

As soon as the employee is able to start performing some or all of his/her duties again then he/she must do so. The employee does not have to wait for instructions to this end. He/she must inform his/her superior immediately of his/her recovery.

## **Staying abroad**

If the employee becomes occupationally disabled whilst abroad then he/she must phone his/her employer immediately upon becoming – and in any case no later than one day after becoming – occupationally disabled, stating his/her accommodation address and phone number. The employee must also inform the competent authority in that country and follow the latter’s instructions. When staying in EU countries, the correct documents must be sent to the employer immediately, who will pass them on to the working conditions service. Immediately upon his/her return home, the employee is obliged to immediately report to his/her employer and hand over a doctor’s certificate specifying the first day of the illness, the nature of the illness, the treatment provided, and the reason for occupational disability.

 The employee is prohibited from going on holiday whilst occupationally disabled unless the employer has consented to this.

## **Cooperating with the rehabilitation process**

Under the Eligibility for Permanent Invalidity Benefit (Restrictions) Act, the employee is obliged to cooperate with the drafting and implementation of an Action Plan that the employer must draw up after eight weeks of sickness absence. The employee – together with his/her employer and the working conditions service – is responsible for making active efforts to recover and rehabilitate and for the periodic evaluation of these measures. If necessary, he/she should address these matters with his/her employer.

Under the Eligibility for Permanent Invalidity Benefit (Restrictions) Act, two weeks after receiving the working conditions service intervention advice (which is provided during the sixth week) the employer is obliged to draw up an Action Plan, to appoint a case manager, to make a maximum effort to achieve the ill employee’s rehabilitation, to intervene in order to help achieve recovery and rehabilitation, to offer adjusted or appropriate work, and to maintain a detailed rehabilitation file from the commencement of the illness onwards. If the absence lasts longer than 37 weeks then the employer must give the employee a copy of the rehabilitation report. The working conditions service must also provide a copy of its rehabilitation report, including medical information, to the employee in a timely manner. 

These rehabilitation reports include the views of all three of these parties regarding the quality of the working relationship. The employee must submit all the rehabilitation reports when applying for a benefit under the Work and Income (Ability to Work) Act (*Wet werk en inkomen naar arbeidsvermogen*, or WIA). If desired, the employer and employee can jointly decide to apply to the UWV for a delay to the WIA phase.

## **Carrying out work**

During his/her occupational disability, the employee must not perform any work, apart from work offered to him/her by or on behalf of the employer. If the employee fails to perform the (replacement or other) work offered without a sound reason then the employer is entitled to refuse to carry on paying his/her wages during the illness. Work that is deemed to be appropriate must be accepted, with possible sanctions for refusing it including stopping the continued payment of wages during illness and even requesting the employee’s dismissal via the Centre for Work and Income (CWI) or the subdistrict court. The replacement work offered will be decided on in consultation with the medical officer. If necessary, a second opinion may be obtained from the UWV. As of 1 January 2003, an employer must also endeavor to achieve the employee’s rehabilitation at another employer, if necessary. The employee must cooperate with these efforts.

## **Labor conflict**

The Eligibility for Permanent Invalidity Benefit (Restrictions) Act expects both employer and employee to take an active approach to the resolution of a labor problem or conflict. The working conditions service must state in writing no later than in the sixth week of the illness whether there is a labor conflict, and put forward proposals to resolve it. In the rehabilitation report (drawn up after 37 weeks of illness), the three parties must each state their views on the quality of the working relationship. If the employee does not cooperate with the conflict mediation or other mediation process then the employer may suspend the payment of his/her wages. In addition, the UWV can impose sanctions on the employee and employer.

## **Second opinion**

If an employee disagrees with the advice given by the working conditions service then this must be reported to the employer immediately. If the working conditions service medical officer agrees with the advice given then the employer will make a decision regarding the continued payment of wages during illness.

 The difference of opinion may for instance relate to (un)suitability for work, the existence of suitable work, suitability of work offered, and the rehabilitation efforts made by the employer and/or the working conditions service. The employee can then ask the UWV to give a second opinion on any or all of these matters. The medical officer will state how and where to get in touch with the UWV. The costs of a second opinion may be charged to the party who asks for this second opinion. The employer can also ask for a second opinion in the above situations. In some cases, this may be a requirement under the Eligibility for Permanent Invalidity Benefit (Restrictions) Act.

## **Sanctions**

If the Occupational Health Services holds that the above verification rules have been breached, it will inform the employer. If the provisions of these rules have been breached, then the employer is entitled to impose sanctions on the employee.

The following sanctions apply in the case of:

- reporting sick too late: verbal warning;
- unjustified stay abroad: written warning;
- breaching the verification rules: written warning.

Furthermore, the employer can suspend or stop the continued payment of wages during illness if the employee fails to cooperate sufficiently with any of the following:

- the drafting of an Action Plan;
- recovery and rehabilitation efforts (or with interventions to this end);
- periodic evaluations;
- the rehabilitation report;
- the acceptance of suitable work at the employee’s employer;
- the acceptance of suitable work at another employer.

If insufficient efforts have been made, then the UWV can impose a wage sanction of up to one year on the employer or else refuse to grant the employee in question the WIA benefit
